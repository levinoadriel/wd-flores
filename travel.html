Travel Info

About Bali

Bali, also known as Island of the Gods, stakes a serious claim to be paradise on earth and has something to offer a very broad market of visitors whether you are a bachelor, family or a wedding guest!

How to get to Bali

Whether you will be coming from Europe, Asia or other parts of the world, we recommend you to use Skyscanner, a global search engine for flights.

Bali has one airport more known as Denpasar International Airport. Airport code (IATA): DPS

Visa

For overseas guests, Indonesian and some Asia countries (including Singapore and Hong Kong does not require Visa). Visa on Arrival (for our European guests) will be eligible for Visa On Arrival (VoA), US$ 35 / person for 30 days.

Local Currency

The national currency in Bali is Indonesia Rupiah (IDR). Most banks will exchange currency during normal business hours and charge a service fee.

Getting Around

If you plan to explore more than a hotel pool you do need a method to get around in Bali, as the island is fairly large. Unless you are not keen to rent a car/motorbike and drive yourself, we recommend you to rent a car with a driver (Ranges from IDR 300.00– 600.000 IDR (US$ 20 – 50/ day)
